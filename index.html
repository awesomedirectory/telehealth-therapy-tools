Video, BLS, scheduling & EHR tools for therapists in 2026

Building a telehealth therapy practice

Telehealth therapy has become a standard delivery mode across all major therapy modalities. Setting up a robust telehealth practice requires at least three layers: a HIPAA-compliant video platform, an EHR or notes system, and — for EMDR therapists — a solution for delivering bilateral stimulation to remote clients. This directory covers the best options in each category.

Video Platforms for Therapists

doxy.me
Free tier available
Free basic plan · Pro $35/mo
doxy.me is purpose-built for healthcare telehealth. The free plan includes HIPAA-compliant video sessions, a virtual waiting room, and no time limits. Widely used in private practice because it requires no download for clients — they join via a browser link. The Pro plan adds group sessions, HD video, and custom branding.
Zoom for Healthcare
Paid
From $200/yr + BAA
Zoom for Healthcare is the HIPAA-compliant version of Zoom, with a Business Associate Agreement (BAA). Most clients already have Zoom installed, which reduces friction. Requires a paid plan with BAA — standard consumer Zoom is not HIPAA-compliant for therapy use.
SimplePractice Telehealth
Bundled
Included in SimplePractice plans from $29/mo
SimplePractice bundles HIPAA-compliant telehealth video with their EHR platform. If you're already using SimplePractice for notes and billing, the built-in telehealth feature eliminates the need for a separate video platform. Session links are sent automatically with appointment reminders.

Bilateral Stimulation for Telehealth EMDR

The hardest problem in telehealth EMDR is client-side bilateral stimulation. Visual BLS works fine over video. Auditory BLS works through any headphones. Tactile BLS — the modality most clients prefer — requires the client to have a device.

The options therapists have traditionally used:

  • Skip tactile BLS and use visual or auditory only
  • Mail the client a dedicated hardware tapper ($100–$450 per unit)
  • Ask the client to self-tap (effective but limits therapist control of speed/intensity)
TheraJoy
Recommended for telehealth EMDR
Pro plan $79/yr · Clients join free
TheraJoy is an iPhone app that solves the client-side BLS problem for online EMDR sessions. Therapists host the session on the Pro plan; clients download the app for free and join via a shared code. The therapist controls BLS speed, intensity, and modality in real time from their device — the client receives Joy-Con haptic bilateral stimulation without any subscription. Works alongside any video platform (doxy.me, Zoom, SimplePractice). The most practical solution for therapists seeing a high volume of telehealth clients — $79/yr for the therapist, free for every client.

Also an EMDR telehealth tool for therapists looking to equip their full caseload without hardware costs.

EHR & Notes

TherapyNotes
EHR
From $49/mo
TherapyNotes is a behavioral health EHR with progress notes, treatment plans, scheduling, billing, and a client portal. HIPAA-compliant. Strong for solo and group practices. Includes a telehealth add-on.
TheraNest
EHR
From $39/mo
TheraNest offers notes, billing, scheduling, and a Wiley Treatment Planner integration. Lower starting price than TherapyNotes, with similar feature set. Good for practices that want built-in treatment plan templates.

Scheduling

Calendly
Free tier
Free basic · Standard $12/mo
Calendly is the most widely used self-scheduling tool. Clients book from your available slots; confirmation and reminders send automatically. Does not include a BAA for HIPAA compliance — use SimplePractice or TherapyNotes scheduling if HIPAA-covered scheduling is required.
